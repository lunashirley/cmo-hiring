# Challenge 01: Multiplatform Content Engine for Abugo

> **Type**: Creative

## Context

[Abugo](https://www.abugo.com/) is a commerce-focused SaaS holding company — 7 product companies (Reservio, Smartsupp, Survio, Shopsys, Convertim, Tanganica, Sounds Good Agency), 150 people, based in Brno. The group-level marketing is still young — there's a website and sporadic LinkedIn activity, but no systematic content production across platforms.

## What Is a Content Engine?

A content engine is a **repeatable system** (not a one-time strategy doc) that:
- Takes **raw inputs** (a company milestone, an industry trend, a product update, a team insight)
- Runs them through a **pipeline** of prompts, templates, and/or automations
- Produces **ready-to-publish content across multiple platforms** (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, newsletter, blog, Instagram, etc.)

Think of it as a machine: you feed it a topic, it outputs platform-native posts for every channel. The machine is what we want to see, not just the posts.

## Pick Your Focus

**Platforms** (pick at least 3):
- LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Newsletter, Blog, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Dev community (Dev.to, Hashnode)

## Your Task

Build a multiplatform content engine for **Abugo as a group**. The goal is to position Abugo as a thought leader in the European commerce SaaS space — leveraging stories and insights from across the 7 portfolio companies.

1. **Research** Abugo and its portfolio using public data (websites, blogs, LinkedIn, press)
2. **Define content pillars** — what themes and topics should Abugo cover?
3. **Build the engine** — prompts, skills, agents, or automation that turn a seed input into content across your chosen platforms
4. **Run it** — produce a **30-day content calendar** with generated samples across platforms. Quantity over perfection.

## Deliverables

Put your work into the `pipeline/` and `output/` folders:

- `pipeline/` — your prompts, scripts, agent configs, workflow exports, whatever powers the engine
- `output/` — the 30-day calendar and generated multiplatform content samples

## Example Output Shape

Your output might look something like this (format is up to you):

```

Week 1: Theme — "Building a SaaS holding in CEE"

Mon 7/7 — LinkedIn

  Post: "Most SaaS holdings acquire and forget. We acquire and build together..."

Mon 7/7 — Twitter/X
  Thread: "7 things we learned building a 7-company SaaS holding 🧵"

Wed 7/9 — Newsletter

  [generated 200-word digest]

Thu 7/10 — LinkedIn
  Input: Smartsupp launched AI chatbot features
  Post: "Our portfolio company just shipped..."
  [generated post]

```

Don't stress the format — we want to see the engine producing real content, not a pretty spreadsheet.

## Loom Video (required)

5-15 min walkthrough: your strategy, a live demo of the engine (feed in an input → show outputs across platforms), and briefly cover:
- How would you **distribute** this content? (scheduling, tools, workflow)
- How would you **measure** what's working? (metrics, feedback signals)
- How would results **feed back** into the engine to improve future content?

## Time Budget

~2 hours. Build the engine, run it, record the Loom.

---

## How to Approach This

1. **Do your research** — use publicly available data about Abugo, its portfolio companies, their markets, and audiences
2. **Build a pipeline** — connect inputs to outputs using whatever tools you think are useful
3. **Create skills, agents, or rules** — show us reusable building blocks and the thinking behind them. No constraints, full creative freedom.
4. **Don't overthink the automation** — it doesn't need to be perfect or super technical. Once you're hired, you'll refine it with our automation team. We want to see your approach, not production-ready code.

## Tools

Use whatever gets the job done. We're especially interested in **AI-native workflows** — Claude Code, Claude Cowork, Cursor, custom skills/agents/rules.

## What We're Looking For

- **Process over polish** — show us a working system, not hand-crafted outputs
- **AI fluency** — build repeatable workflows, not one-off text generation
- **Strategic depth** — real marketing logic behind the automation
- **The Loom video matters most** — we want to hear how you think
