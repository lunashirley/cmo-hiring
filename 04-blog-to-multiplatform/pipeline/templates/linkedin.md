## LinkedIn Post Template

### Structural constraints
- **Length:** 150–300 words (recommended); hard cap 3,000 characters
- **Paragraphs:** 3–6 short paragraphs. Single sentences on their own line are fine — encourages "see more" clicks.
- **Hashtags:** 0–3, placed at the very end of the post. Never mid-body.
- **Emojis:** Optional. Use sparingly (max 3 per post) and only if the brand voice allows them.
- **Links:** Do not embed URLs in the body. If referencing a link, note it in a "Link in comments →" line at the end.

### Structural pattern
```
[HOOK — 1 sentence, standalone, pattern-interrupt]

[SETUP — 1-2 sentences establishing why this matters]

[BODY — 2-4 short paragraphs delivering atoms: stats, insights, or examples]

[CLOSE — 1 sentence CTA, question, or reflection that invites response]

[optional: #hashtag1 #hashtag2]
```

### Minimum atom requirements
- At least 2 atoms clearly represented in the post
- At least 1 stat OR insight atom (not only anecdotes)

### Tone register
- Professional but human. Not a press release.
- First person preferred. Plural "we" allowed for company accounts.
- Specific > general. "Our support volume dropped 34%" beats "we saw improvement."
