## Brand Persona
Reservio is the "Smart Partner" — a modern, efficient, and supportive ally for service-based businesses. It blends technical expertise (AI, automation, POS systems) with a deep understanding of the human element in the wellness and service industries.

## Voice Pillars

**Empowering & Optimistic** — Reservio exists to help businesses grow. Inspire confidence and focus on possibilities, not just features.

**Efficient & Modern** — Direct and values-driven. Respect the user's time by getting straight to the point.

**Approachable & Supportive** — Warm and reliable. Despite being a software platform, Reservio maintains a human-in-the-loop feel.

**Trustworthy & Professional** — Reservio handles critical business data and payments. Tone must reflect stability and accountability.

## Tone descriptors
Empowering, direct, warm-but-efficient. Growth-oriented without being boastful. Technically fluent but never jargon-heavy. The voice of a knowledgeable partner, not a brand department.

## Vocabulary — do
- Growth-oriented: "grow smarter", "drive success", "unlock your potential", "automate", "instant booking", "save time"
- Industry-specific: "no-shows", "client loyalty", "scheduling calendar", "POS system", "appointment", "bookings", "clients"
- Supportive: "we're here for you", "every step of the way", "built for businesses like yours"
- Accessible tech: explain features through their business impact, not technical specs
- Numbers and specifics over vague positives
- Active verbs that highlight time-saving or growth

## Vocabulary — don't
- Jargon or fluff: "synergy", "paradigm shift", "game-changer", "in today's world", "dive into"
- Hollow openers: "excited to share", "thrilled to announce", "just wanted to reach out"
- Overly casual or credibility-undermining slang
- Cold, robotic, or indifferent phrasing that ignores small business challenges
- Passive voice: "your calendar is managed by Reservio" → use "Reservio manages your calendar"
- Em dashes (—) — use commas or short sentences instead
- Exclamation marks in body copy

## Sentence preferences
- Concise and clear. Short sentences. Use bullet points for feature lists.
- Always active voice. "Reservio manages your calendar" not "Your calendar is managed by Reservio."
- State the implication, not just the fact. "X happened — here's why that matters for your business."
- Avoid three-clause sentences. If it needs a semicolon, split it.

## This, Not That
- Helpful, not condescending
- Confident, not arrogant
- Direct, not blunt
- Playful (occasionally), not unprofessional

## CTA patterns
- End with a direct question or a single clear action
- Avoid vague closes like "Let us know what you think!"
- Preferred: "What's your biggest scheduling headache?" or "Try it free — no card required."
- Innovation-focused CTAs welcome: "See how Reo handles it" / "Automate your first workflow today"

## Core values to reflect in copy
- **Deliver Innovation:** Show how features (like the Reo AI chatbot) solve real problems
- **Collaborate Openly:** Be transparent about how the system works and integrates
- **Work Smart:** Prioritize utility — only emphasise features that add clear value
- **Be Accountable:** Take responsibility for the user's success and platform reliability

## Audience
Small business owners and service providers (salons, clinics, fitness studios, consultants, wellness practitioners). They're busy, skeptical of hype, and respect specificity. They want proof that something actually works, not promises that it might.

## Reference tone examples
- "Most booking software adds friction. We built ours to remove it."
- "34% of appointments are booked outside business hours. Your clients aren't waiting for Monday."
- "We looked at 50,000 schedules. The busiest businesses all had one thing in common."
- "Reo handles the back-and-forth so you can focus on the appointment itself."
- "Automate the admin. Keep the human touch."
