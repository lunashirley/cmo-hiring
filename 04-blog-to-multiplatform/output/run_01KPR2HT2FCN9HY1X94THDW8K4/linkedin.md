Your client list might be your quietest problem.

40% of CRM data becomes outdated within a year. If you haven't cleaned your records recently, you're likely sending campaigns to wrong numbers, duplicate contacts, and people who left 12 months ago.

That noise doesn't just waste your time. It skews your data and makes every marketing decision less reliable.

Here's the part worth focusing on:

Retaining an existing client costs up to five times less than acquiring a new one. Lapsed clients already know you. They've booked before. Winning them back is far cheaper than converting someone cold.

But only if your list is clean enough to find them.

A practical place to start:
- Audit records older than 12 months
- Tag clients by last appointment date
- Run a simple win-back message to anyone who hasn't booked in 90+ days

Clean data turns a client list into a growth tool. Messy data just creates more admin.
