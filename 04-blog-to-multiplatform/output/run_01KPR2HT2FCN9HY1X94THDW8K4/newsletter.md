Your client list is probably working against you

---

That client database you built 18 months ago has a problem: 40% of CRM data goes stale within a year. Wrong numbers, duplicate entries, contacts who churned quietly — they're adding noise to every campaign you run and skewing every decision you make about who to reach.

**The fix isn't more outreach. It's cleaner data first.**

Before you spend time or money chasing new clients, it's worth looking at what's already in your list. Retaining an existing client costs up to five times less than acquiring a new one. Lapsed clients already know your service, your space, your team. A targeted win-back message to the right segment of your list will almost always outperform a cold acquisition push.

**Once your list is clean, protect your cash flow at the source.** The most effective change service businesses make isn't a new payment tool — it's collecting a deposit or full payment at the time of booking. No invoice step. No chasing. The money arrives before the appointment does, and your schedule reflects real commitment, not tentative interest.

These three changes — auditing your data, re-engaging lapsed clients, and moving to upfront payment — belong in the same workflow. Each one makes the next more effective.
