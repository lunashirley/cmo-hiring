The average small business loses nearly $10,000 annually due to outdated client data. Cleaning your database isn't just a task—it's a critical step to improve marketing effectiveness and avoid costly mistakes. Here’s how to do it right.

Start by removing duplicate contacts and updating outdated information. This simple step ensures your marketing messages reach the right people—and prevents wasted ad spend. Then, add missing details like birthdays or preferred contact methods. A clean database builds better client relationships.

40% of CRM data becomes outdated within a year, impacting business operations negatively. But outdated contacts aren't just a numbers problem—they cause confusion, missed opportunities, and eroded trust.

Action step: Schedule a dedicated hour this week to clean your client database. Start with a spreadsheet filter to find duplicates. Then, send a targeted email to update missing information.
