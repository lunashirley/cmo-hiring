# Competitive Battlecard: Smartsupp vs. Tidio
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-22 | **Run Type:** manual

## TL;DR (For the Rep in a Hurry)
- **Tidio is cheaper at entry** ($24.17/mo vs. Smartsupp's $17 base, but Smartsupp includes video recordings from day one; Tidio's AI is an add-on like ours).
- **Tidio has broader enterprise credibility** (300K+ customers, brand logos, 4.6/5 from 1,904 G2 reviews) but Smartsupp has tighter e-commerce positioning and unique video-recording behavioral context.
- **Both position on AI autonomy** (67% for Tidio's Lyro vs. 96% for Smartsupp's Mira), but Smartsupp's visual proof differentiator (visitor recordings) is unmatched and critical for complex-product retailers (our ICP's second persona).

### Core Positioning
**Tidio:** "More automation, less 'I hate your support'" — positions as a *help desk + AI alignment* platform emphasizing human-AI collaboration and brand trust.

**Smartsupp:** "Autonomous Revenue Engine for e-commerce" — positions as *conversion accelerator* combining AI autonomy + behavioral intelligence (video recordings).

### Strategic Narrative
**Tidio's play:** "Lyro is the trustworthy AI agent that understands context and acts like a human." Heavy emphasis on *brand voice alignment* and *not replacing teams*. Positioning against over-automation concerns; differentiator is perceived emotional intelligence and safety.

**Smartsupp's play:** "Mira is fully autonomous; visitor video recordings give you the behavioral context no competitor has." Positioning against *friction and incompleteness*; the combination of high autonomy (96%) + visual proof = faster resolution and higher conversion.

**ICP Perception:** *Tidio's messaging appeals to risk-averse enterprises worried about AI damaging brand; Smartsupp's messaging speaks to growth-stage e-shops and complex-product retailers who need speed + proof. For our ICP (scaling without headcount), Smartsupp's video recording + high autonomy is a stronger value fit.*

### Messaging Vulnerability

**Smartsupp:** The video recording feature is underexplained in sales materials; most prospects don't immediately grasp why behavioral context matters for resolution speed. Need to anchor this in ICP pain (complex products, high cart abandonment, rage clicks = lost sales).

**ICP Perception:** *Tidio's "67% resolution rate guarantee" feels safer but lower-ceiling; Smartsupp's 96% autonomy feels ambitious and aligned with ICP goal to scale support without headcount.*

- **Smartsupp advantage:** Higher star rating (4.7 vs. 4.6); proof point in case of close technical call
- **Smartsupp limitation:** Fewer reviews = less social proof for risk-averse buyers; Tidio's volume wins here

**ICP Perception:** *Tidio's review count and 5-star clustering suggest strong product-market fit at SMB level. However, Smartsupp's 4.7/5 with e-commerce case studies (Breno, AAA AUTO) is more relevant to ICP. Tidio's complaint theme "expensive pricing" is a warning signal for mid-market budget sensitivity.*

1. **Lyro AI as managed service** — Premium tier now includes "Lyro AI as a managed service" (dedicated team, guaranteed 50% resolution rate, pay-per-resolution billing)

### Smartsupp's Investment Areas
1. **Video recording as core differentiator** — Consistently positioned as unique, never commoditized
2. **E-commerce ROI focus** — Case studies emphasize conversion rate lift, revenue, lead generation (not just ticket automation)
3. **Omnichannel depth** — WhatsApp, Messenger, email integration tight; positioning as "inbox consolidation" for omnichannel brands

**Implication:** *Tidio is investing in managed services and enterprise ops; Smartsupp is deepening e-commerce-specific autonomy and visual intelligence. Both are moving upmarket, but Tidio's "team management" play may appeal to larger orgs, while Smartsupp's "revenue generation" play is more aligned to mid-market e-commerce growth.*

## Strategic Implications

### Where We Win (Smartsupp)

1. **Visitor Video Recordings (Exclusive)**
   - *Situation:* Complex-product retailer (furniture, auto, high-tech) with high cart abandonment. Rep shows video of prospect rage-clicking product carousel → Smartsupp's Mira AI now has context to resolve (e.g., "carousel is broken on mobile" → refund, upsell, or live agent handoff).
   - *Why Tidio loses:* Lyro has no behavioral context; agent is flying blind.
   - *Messaging:* "See how your customers navigate. Mira sees it too."

2. **96% vs. 67% Autonomy**
   - *Situation:* Growth-stage shop at €8M revenue needs to handle 300+ daily inquiries without hiring 2 more agents.
   - *Smartsupp:* Mira resolves 96% → 288 inquiries auto-resolved, 12 to agent.
   - *Tidio:* Lyro resolves 67% → 201 auto-resolved, 99 to agent → still need +1 headcount.
   - *Messaging:* "Mira isn't an assist layer; it's a fully autonomous agent. We proved 96% with Leader Fox."

3. **Price-to-Power Ratio (Video + AI + Omnichannel from $17)**
   - *Situation:* Budget-conscious SMB wants AI + analytics cheaply.
   - *Smartsupp Solo ($17):* video recordings + live chat + Mira AI add-on.
   - *Tidio Starter ($24.17):* live chat only; Lyro AI is $32.50+ more.
   - *Effective entry cost:* Smartsupp ~$17 (video is the "free" differentiator), Tidio $56.67+ (chat + AI).

4. **E-Commerce ROI (Conversion-Focused)**
   - *Situation:* ICP CFO asks, "What's the revenue impact?"
   - *Smartsupp proof:* Breno +169% conversion, +10.8% revenue; AAA AUTO 7,200 leads/month.
   - *Tidio proof:* Procosmet +27% conversion (case study), but positioning is "scale support," not "drive revenue."
   - *Messaging:* "Every conversation is a conversion opportunity. Here's how we proved it."

### Where We're Vulnerable (Tidio)

1. **Broader Market Credibility & Logo Power**
   - *Why Tidio wins:* 300K+ customers vs. Smartsupp's smaller base; brand names (Under Armour, The Body Shop, Stanley) lend enterprise weight.
   - *ICP impact:* Mid-market CFO may default to "industry standard" if Smartsupp feels niche.
   - *Risk:* Buyer fear of choosing "unknown" vendor; Tidio's G2 review count (1,904 vs. 1,039) amplifies this.

2. **Help Desk Maturity**
   - *Why Tidio wins:* Advanced ticketing, departments, tagging, automation at Growth tier ($49.17). Smartsupp's help desk is basic until Expert ($83).
   - *ICP impact:* If prospect is already using Zendesk or Freshdesk, Tidio feels more like a "complete replacement" (single platform); Smartsupp feels like "live chat + AI add-on."
   - *Risk:* Prospect wants to consolidate support stack; Tidio's Plus tier (multiproject, departments) looks better than Smartsupp's Solo/Plus cap (1 operator, 3 sites).

3. **Managed Service & Enterprise SLA**
   - *Why Tidio wins:* Premium tier includes "Lyro as a managed service" + guaranteed 50% resolution + custom analytics.
   - *ICP impact:* Larger mid-market (€15M+) may prefer "managed service" over self-serve tuning.
   - *Risk:* Smartsupp's Ultimate tier is listed but not heavily emphasized; Tidio's is positioned as a true service offering.

4. **Flows for Conversion (26% Lift)**
   - *Why Tidio wins:* Flows are positioned as a *marketing automation tool*; 26% conversion lift is a strong claim.
   - *ICP impact:* Growth-stage e-shops care about cart recovery and lead gen. Tidio's Flows + proactive engagement competes with Smartsupp's visitor list engagement.
   - *Risk:* Smartsupp's messaging is "turn conversations into conversions"; Tidio's is "Flows increase conversions by 26%" — both claim ROI, but Tidio has a named metric.

### Recommended Moves

#### Sales Messaging Adjustments
1. **Lead with Video + Autonomy combination**, not separately
   - Weak: "We have visitor video recordings."
   - Strong: "You'll see exactly why customers abandon carts (video), and Mira will auto-resolve 96% of inquiries without your team. That's the speed complex-product retailers need."
   
2. **Anchor on e-commerce ROI, not support efficiency**
   - Weak: "We deflect 96% of tickets."
   - Strong: "Breno increased conversion rate by 169% and revenue by 10.8%. Here's how: every conversation becomes a data point (video + chat), Mira resolves it autonomously, and your agents focus on high-value upsells."

3. **Address Tidio's brand credibility head-on**
   - Objection: "Tidio has 300K+ customers."
   - Response: "Tidio is broad; we're focused. Our 1,000+ customers are e-commerce retailers with complex products — furniture, auto parts, high-tech. Look at Breno's results. That's who we're built for."

4. **Emphasize video in complex-product scenarios**
   - When: Prospect sells furniture, automotive, apparel with customization.
   - Message: "Tidio's AI works best with text FAQs. Your customers' problems are visual — wrong color shipped, size mismatch, assembly issues. Mira sees the video and resolves 96% without back-and-forth."
