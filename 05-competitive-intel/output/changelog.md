# Competitive Intelligence Changelog

## 2026-04-22 | manual

### What Changed
- **Tidio Premium tier:** New managed-service offering (Lyro as managed service, guaranteed 50% resolution, pay-per-resolution billing) signals upmarket expansion; shifts narrative from "DIY platform" to "operational partner." Previous battlecard did not address this.
- **Tidio's Agentic AI narrative:** Blog heavily features "generative AI agents," positioning Lyro as enterprise automation play (vs. pure customer support). Increases competitive positioning against Zendesk, not just live chat.
- **Smartsupp's video recordings:** Confirmed still unmatched in market; no competitive response from Tidio. Positioning stability suggests this remains Smartsupp's primary moat.
- **G2 review dynamics:** Tidio now at 1,904 reviews (vs. Smartsupp's 1,039), +12% from previous baseline. Tidio's 80% five-star clustering remains strong; Smartsupp's 4.7/5 rating holds but volume gap widens.

### Stable (No Material Change)
- **Core AI autonomy claims:** Tidio 67%, Smartsupp 96%—gap and messaging unchanged.
- **Pricing structure:** Smartsupp Solo $17, Tidio Starter $24.17; Plus tier cliff remains ($83 vs. $749). No new pricing moves detected.
- **Help desk maturity:** Tidio's Growth tier remains stronger than Smartsupp's Plus tier; no Smartsupp help desk roadmap expansion signaled.
- **Omnichannel coverage:** Both platforms cover WhatsApp, Messenger, email; Tidio adds Instagram; Smartsupp emphasizes e-commerce integration. No material shift.
- **Case study evidence:** Smartsupp's Breno (+169% conversion) and AAA AUTO (7,200 leads/month) remain primary ROI proof; Tidio's Procosmet (+27% conversion) and Flows (26% lift) remain positioning anchors.

### ICP Implication
**Tidio's premium-tier managed service is a direct threat to Smartsupp's Ultimate tier pitch.** Mid-market (€10M–€20M) teams now see Tidio offering "guaranteed 50% resolution + managed tuning," which removes operational burden of Mira configuration. This shifts ICP buying trigger from "we need autonomy" to "we need partner-backed delivery." **Recommended counter:** Emphasize that Smartsupp's 96% autonomy *requires less operational tuning* than Lyro's 67% (less handoff = less training data needed). Lead with e-commerce ROI (Breno +10.8% revenue) as proof that Smartsupp's autonomy-first approach delivers faster business impact than Tidio's "managed service" safety net. For ICP's risk-averse CFO, position Smartsupp as "proven in your vertical" vs. Tidio as "enterprise approach that may overshoot your team's complexity." Identify persona early: if CFO wants "vendor manages AI risk," Tidio's premium wins; if Revenue/Growth leader wants "fast conversion lift with minimal overhead," Smartsupp wins.

## 2026-04-22 | manual

### What Changed
- **First run — no prior baseline.** Tidio competitive profile established across pricing, product features, G2 sentiment, strategic positioning, and customer proof.
- **Key findings:** Tidio claims 67% AI autonomy vs. Smartsupp's 96%; Tidio has 65% more G2 reviews (1,904 vs. 1,039) but Smartsupp has higher rating (4.7 vs. 4.6) and stronger e-commerce case studies. Tidio's video recordings remain unmatched in market.
- **Pricing discovery:** Tidio entry is $24.17; Smartsupp $17 (+ video differentiation). True comparison (chat + AI) favors Smartsupp at mid-market tier (Expert $83 vs. Tidio Plus $749).

### Stable (No Material Change)
- Smartsupp's core positioning (autonomous revenue engine + behavioral context) vs. Tidio's positioning (human-AI alignment + brand trust) are materially different narratives.
- Omnichannel capabilities (WhatsApp, Messenger, email) are feature-parity; no Tidio advantage.
- G2 rating differential (4.7 vs. 4.6) is marginal; Tidio's review volume is material credibility signal.

### ICP Implication
For mid-market e-commerce retailers (€5M–€20M revenue), Tidio is a credible, easier-to-justify alternative on headline price and brand recognition. However, Smartsupp's video recordings + 96% autonomy + e-commerce focus (Breno, AAA AUTO) is a stronger fit for growth-stage shops and complex-product retailers managing high-volume support and cart abandonment. Sales must lead with e-commerce ROI and behavioral differentiation, not just "autonomous agent" claims, to overcome Tidio's broader market presence and lower perceived entry cost.
